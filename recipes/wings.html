Grilled Party Wings

Description

Grilled party wings are always a crowd favorite. Grilling them creates a nice charred taste, and if done correctly, can leave them with a scrumptious crunchy skin. The skin won't be as crunchy as fried wings, but they will still satisfy everyone who enjoys a bit of crunch to their wings.

Ingredients

  • 2 Pounds Party Wings
  • Olive Oil
  • Freshly ground salt
  • Freshly ground pepper
  • Favorite Buffalo or BBQ sauce of your choice

Directions

  1. Heat grill to 350-400 degrees F
  2. While grill is heating, place wings in a large bowl. Cover wings in about 4 tablespoons of olive oil. Toss the wings to evenly coat each wing. Using a fresh pepper grinder, generously pepper the wings. Add salt to taste. Freshly ground salt works best.
  3. Add wings to grill. Keep wings close together. Space in between will decrease their juicineess.
  4. Grill wings for 25-30 minutes, turning the wings every 5 minutes.
  5. Toward the end of the time frame thinner wings will be done a bit before the thicker ones. Use a meat thermometer to ensure each wing is 165 degree F. Place smaller wings that are done earlier in a bowl, while keeping a close eye on the larger ones.
  6. Once all the wings are finished cooking, place them in a large bowl. Toss the wings in your sauce of choice. I always prefer a generous amount of sauce, but add sauce to your own personal preference
  7. Time to eat the wings! Grilling wings can take some learning as cooking time can vary per grill. Be patient and practice makes perfect!
